Capybaras!

Capybaras, the world's largest rodents, are fascinating creatures. They're semiaquatic, often found lounging in water to regulate their body temperature. Despite their size, they're excellent swimmers and can stay submerged for up to five minutes! Social animals, they form close-knit groups and communicate through various vocalizations, including purring. Their unique dental structure allows them to graze on aquatic plants, making them essential for maintaining wetland ecosystems. With webbed feet and a laid-back demeanor, capybaras are both quirky and crucial to their habitats.
